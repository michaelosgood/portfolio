About Me

My name is Michael Osgood and I am a huge science and tech enthusiast. I have completed an intensive 24-week Full-Stack Coding Bootcamp through the University of California, Irvine and I work as a Quality Assurance Tester for PrescribeWellness, an innovative software company in Irvine, California.

To elaborate on my past, I worked in Public Health for the U.S Air Force from 2007 to 2011. I was stationed south of Trenton, New Jersey at McGuire Air Force Base. As a Public Health Tech, my overall responsibility was to protect the local population from environmental threats through preventative measures. This career field spurred my fascination with Biology and Preventative Medicine.

After four years in the Air Force, I decided to pursue a Biology degree. So I moved to Boulder, Colorado and studied Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at the University of Colorado, Boulder. During that time, I studied a myriad of topics to include Evolution, Genetics, Ecology, Chemistry, and Physics. Also, I started working in Client Support as a Support Analyst for VisionLink, a software company in Boulder, Colorado. After completing my bachelors, I moved to California and resumed working on the client-facing side of software for PrescribeWellness. After 2 years of working on the client-facing side of software, I transitioned into my current role as Quality Assurance Tester.

In my tenure, I have gained valuable experience from assisting clients on the client-facing side to collaborating with the development team to resolve bugs on the development side. Not only that, I understand how frustrating using poorly-designed software can affect the end-user. This knowledge I accumulated working with software is going to help me become a better developer. Furthermore, I gained crucial knowledge in developing and testing software through the coding boot camp. The coursework covered many popular topics including: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, React.js, MySQL and many other tools.
